## Recommendation

Scenario A is recommended despite carrying more flagged assumptions, because the cost advantage is substantial ($254K lower over 3 years) and the ROI differential is large (3.27x vs 1.63x). Both scenarios share the same critical unvalidated assumptions (fabricated 45% conversion lift exceeding the 32% benchmark ceiling, and an unverifiable operating budget), so neither branch has a clean risk profile. Scenario A's additional output sanity check flags that both ROI and payback may be understated — this is a cost-validation concern, not a value-overstatement risk, and it applies equally to Scenario B's payback check. The hosting architecture assumption is also unvalidated for both branches. Recommendation confidence is moderate: both branches rely on a fabricated conversion-lift claim and an unverifiable operating budget, and both produce payback periods below the 6-month realistic threshold — suggesting cost estimates should be validated before committing capital.
